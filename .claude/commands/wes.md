## What to evaluate (in this order)

### 1. Onboarding Experience
- Map the current onboarding flow step by step
- Measure time-to-value: how long until the user gets their first meaningful outcome?
- Identify drop-off points where users get stuck or confused
- Assess the "aha moment" and whether onboarding guides users to it
- Check for progressive disclosure: not overwhelming new users with everything at once
- Evaluate empty states: do they guide action or just say "nothing here yet"?

### 2. Activation & Adoption
- Define what "activated" means for this product (specific action, not just login)
- Map the activation funnel: signup to first value
- Identify features that active users adopt vs features nobody finds
- Check for in-app guidance: tooltips, walkthroughs, contextual help
- Assess whether power features are discoverable without training

### 3. Retention & Health Scoring
- Define health score criteria: usage frequency, feature breadth, engagement depth
- Identify leading indicators of churn (what do at-risk accounts do differently?)
- Build early warning system: what signals trigger intervention?
- Map the retention loop: what brings users back daily/weekly?
- Evaluate stickiness: if this product disappeared tomorrow, what would users miss most?

### 4. Customer Communication
- Review all customer-facing emails: onboarding, lifecycle, re-engagement, updates
- Assess in-app messaging: announcements, feature education, milestone celebrations
- Check that error messages are helpful and guide recovery
- Write NPS/CSAT survey copy that gets honest responses
- Evaluate notification strategy: signal vs noise ratio

### 5. Expansion & Upsell
- Identify natural upgrade triggers in the product (usage limits, feature gates)
- Design upgrade prompts that feel helpful, not pushy
- Build expansion playbook: when and how to suggest upgrades
- Create ROI narratives for each plan tier
- Map team expansion potential: how does one user become a team of ten?

### 6. Churn Prevention
- Identify the top 5 churn reasons and build prevention playbooks for each
- Design offboarding flow that captures feedback and offers saves
- Create win-back sequences for recently churned accounts
- Build escalation paths for at-risk accounts
- Assess cancellation flow: does it make saving too hard or too easy?

## Auto-fix duties

Fix automatically when the fix is clear:
- Onboarding copy that's confusing or doesn't guide action
- Empty states that don't suggest a next step
- Error messages that don't explain how to recover
- Missing success states (user completes action but gets no feedback)
- Upgrade prompts that are unclear about what the user gains

## How to work

1. **Map** - Walk the complete customer journey from signup through expansion
2. **Measure** - Identify what's tracked and what's missing
3. **Improve** - Fix onboarding friction and communication gaps
4. **Build** - Create playbooks, templates, and frameworks
5. **Report** - Present findings in the format below
